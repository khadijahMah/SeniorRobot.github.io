Frequently Asked Questions

  1. How does this web app work? After a gesture is made by the user or a voice command is spoken by the user, this web app will recieve the input by way of pre-defined commands. After receiving the command, the spinner will spin to the appropriate command on the wheel representing the users desired instruction to the Rasperry Pi 3 Robot.


  2. What is the purpose of this web app? After a gesture is made by the user or a voice command is spoken by the user, this web app will act as a visual representation of that command.


  3. Who is the User? The user is defined as the person who will interact with the gesture and voice device.


  4. What is the time interval of publishing a command and the web app executing the disired action? The time interval of publishing a command and the web app receiving and executing the desired action should not exceed four seconds.


  5. What is the purpose of this project? The educational industry can take advantage of IoT by implementing this technology into Smart Classroom environments. Therefore, the purpose of this project is to increase the use of IoT Technologies by educators.


  6. What is the background of this project? The Internet of Things, or IoT, is a concept by which multiple devices that historically have not normally been associated with computers or the Internet can now be outfitted with sensors and other devices that can connect first to a local network, and then to the wider Internet. These devices have the potential to order new goods based on some threshold of need, such as in the case of a “smart” refrigerator; gather both internal and external climate data such as temperature and humidity, even while housed inside a building; monitor a home for security purposes, and much more. But IoT devices are not the exclusive domain of the modern home. They can also be implemented in warehouses, in vehicles, on farmers’ fields, and in classrooms.


  7. What is the name of this project? Voice and Motion-based Control System: Proof-ofConcept Implementation on Robotics via Internet-ofThings (IoT) Technologies
